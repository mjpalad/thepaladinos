<p>I won’t even mention how long its been since my last blog post.&#160; <img class="wlEmoticon wlEmoticon-smile" style="border-top-style: none; border-bottom-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-left-style: none" alt="Smile" src="/assets/images/wlEmoticon-smile.png" />&#160; This week Gabby was assigned an Arkansas diorama project.&#160; She decided to go with one of our local battlefields and we settled on Prairie Grove.&#160; It’s beautiful and one of the most intact Civil War battlefields in the nation.&#160; You can do some walk through and some drive through with a narrative cd.&#160; The grounds are so beautiful and well maintained.&#160; We need to plan on going back in the fall when the apple orchard is open (which is original to the site) and you can pick apples.&#160;&#160;&#160; </p>  <p>After our tour we made a stop at The Briar Rose in Farmington.&#160; It was the cutest bakery/café with a Sleeping Beauty theme.&#160; Wood fire pizzas, sandwiches, espresso and amazing baked goodies.&#160;
